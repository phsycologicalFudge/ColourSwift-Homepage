Void Particle Classification: V₁

Known officially as V₁ Particles, these subatomic fragments are the foundational carriers of Void resonance in biological and synthetic forms. Unlike any element on the standard periodic table, V₁s behave as quantum-bonding anomalies, reacting with traditional matter on a molecular level. They do not exist naturally on Earth—only in regions saturated with Void exposure.

When stabilized, V₁s bind to existing atoms—most commonly carbon, silicon, and calcium—creating advanced Voidborne Compounds. These fusions drastically enhance the tensile strength, energy retention, and elemental resistance of the host material.

Key Compounds

1. VX-Ceramite (V₁ + reinforced carbon lattice)

Used in the armour plating of D.S.O. agent uniforms, VX-Ceramite is flexible, energy-resistant, and capable of reactive Void deflection. It retains molecular cohesion even under sustained Void contact.

2. VX-Myosin (V₁ + organic myosin proteins)

Formed naturally in Void-exposed operatives, this compound strengthens muscle fibre, enhances neural conductivity, and increases reflex compression tolerance. While extremely effective, VX-Myosin has shown signs of psychogenic interference under prolonged use.

3. Aetherite (V₁ raw mineral form)

A naturally occurring crystalline matrix found at Void exposure sites. Aetherite is unstable, and used primarily in controlled refinement to extract trace V₁ for artificial infusion.

Biological Integration

In certain individuals, especially those with an Emphetripia Heart, V₁ particles embed themselves in skeletal muscle, nerve clusters, and even bone marrow, becoming part of the subject’s bio-architecture. This integration is what allows select agents to survive high-grade Void encounters, though side effects vary—ranging from cellular decay to full Void corruption in extreme cases.
